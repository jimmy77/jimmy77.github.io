Got blocked on a simple problem

Thinking back I recall struggling a lot with setting up my blog. It felt like the hand holding had dropped away and all of a sudden I was just left to my own devices.

I think I missed the familiar setup of question/ answer approach that freecodecamp was. With building my blog, there was no button to click to ‘Get a hint’.

I recall feeling dread I felt and I remember feeling out of my depth during that process.

Luckily googling, taking breaks and lots of revising of freecodecamp lessons helped me get there in the end. Using the dev tools in Google Chrome really helped alot.

I spent a lot time using it trying to work out why elements were behaving in unexpected ways. Like working out where headings were inheriting their properties from and how to change that.

The big lesson from the was, plan, plan plan. And then research, research. And don’t panic.

Solved a problem in an elegant way.

So I had a bit of text in my footer that was inheriting my global p tag style. By default it was black and I had intended for my body copy to appear on a white background.

The problem arose when I need to add text to my footer that was going to need a p tag applied.

I struggled at first with find a solution.

Luckily help was only a couple of youtube’s and google searches away and I managed to work out that needed to apply some CSS specificity with my styles. I ended up writing footer specific p tag that made the text white so it was legible against the dark background of my footer.

The takeaway from that was, read and google and look around. The solution will generally be out there.

solving techniques and process:

Pseudocode

For me, this technique is a must and it has really helped me out. I found myself bouncing a little between talking to an imaginary duck( the Rubber ducky method) before writing out some pseudocode. For me, I like the idea of writing out in plain English how I’d approach solving a problem and then afterwards going through the pseudo code and swapping it out for actual code. That to me makes total sense and has helped me understand what I need to do to a degree.

Trying something

Definitely doing a lot of this in between writing pseudocode.

Rubber ducky method

Yep, this in conjunction with pseudo has helped me.

Reading error messages

Yes but definitely realise I need to do this more of especially after re-reading error messages and realising that I’m being literally told how to solve a problem.
